Request for Prayers – دعا کی درخواست

In the name of Allah, The Most Gracious, The Most Merciful

Introduction

This website reflects on the meaning and beauty of requesting and offering prayers (duas) in Islam.

Why We Ask for Prayers

When someone says "Remember me in your prayers," it's a plea for spiritual support. Prayer becomes a bridge between hearts and a connection with the Divine.

The Power of Supplication

Even when destiny is written, prayers can be the channel Allah uses to bring relief. Our role is to pray sincerely and wait, trusting in His wisdom.

Pray. Believe. Wait.

Surah Al-Fatiha

بِسْمِ اللَّهِ الرَّحْمَـٰنِ الرَّحِيمِ
ٱلْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ ٱلْعَـٰلَمِينَ
ٱلرَّحْمَـٰنِ ٱلرَّحِيمِ
مَـٰلِكِ يَوْمِ ٱلدِّينِ
إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ وَإِيَّاكَ نَسْتَعِينُ
ٱهْدِنَا ٱلصِّرَٰطَ ٱلْمُسْتَقِيمَ
صِرَٰطَ ٱلَّذِينَ أَنْعَمْتَ عَلَيْهِمْ
غَيْرِ ٱلْمَغْضُوبِ عَلَيْهِمْ وَلَا ٱلضَّآلِّينَ

This is the core supplication every Muslim repeats daily. It's a humble request for divine guidance and mercy.

Conclusion

Prayer connects us to each other and to Allah. It’s not only about asking—but also about trusting. Let us pray with sincerity and humility, and support one another in supplication.

Alhamdulillah – Allah gives us what we need, even when we don’t know we need it.
